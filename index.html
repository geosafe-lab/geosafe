Abstract

While the rapid progress of generative AI (Gen-AI) and large language models (LLMs) holds great promise for geospatial applications, it also increases privacy, security, and ethical risks. Hence, there is a need for datasets and benchmarks to evaluate Geo-AI systems for safety, reliability, and alignment with human values. Here, we propose a novel GeoSAFE - Geospatial Safety Assurance Framework and Evaluation, based on a new safety taxonomy for geospatial data comprising of six critical hazard categories notably used in geospatial applications. Moreover, GeoSAFE includes GeoSAFE-Dataset, a Geo-AI safety dataset comprising of 11694 samples of LLM-human prompts and explanations adhering to the proposed taxonomy, built on data-centric AI concepts. These concepts ensure fairness assessment based on bias, toxicity, sentiment and ambiguity, thus adhering to Responsible AI (RAI) principles. We present a zero-shot evaluation of existing general purpose AI-safety LLMs, benchmarking their performance in identifying safe/unsafe prompts. Additionally, we present GeoSAFEGuard, an instruction-tuned LLM using the GeoSAFE-Dataset, demonstrating improved performance over zero-shot evaluation. While most general LLMs have high (>20\%) False Unsafe Rate, GeoSAFE attains a 4.6\% False Unsafe Rate, 0.4\% False Safe Rate, and 97\% F1-score on GeoSAFE-Dataset. Finally, we perform an anonymous user-survey for assessing human-GeoSAFE alignment.

Introduction

Safe and trustworthy use of Gen-AI virtual assistants powered by Large Language Models (LLMs) is one of the primary objectives for achieving Responsible-AI (RAI). Recently, significant advances have been made in developing benchmarks capable of evaluating LLMs based on hazard taxonomy. However, these developments have primarily focused on general LLMs, and new approaches are needed to extend these capabilities across other specialized domains. This need arises particularly for guarding against risks involved with domain-specific applications, which are often overlooked or deemed irrelevant by general LLM-based systems.

Key Ideas

  • A taxonomy of Geo-AI related hazards extending the MLCommons V0.5 taxonomy, categorizing potential risks in geospatial AI applications
  • GeoSAFE-Dataset - A prompt and responses dataset consisting of safe and unsafe prompts along with the corresponding explanations and chat responses
  • GeoSAFEGuard - An AI-safety LLM instruction-tuned on the above dataset for classifying text prompts adhering to the proposed Geo-AI hazard taxonomy
  • Evaluation of the human-LLM alignment of the GeoSAFEGuard model

Conclusion

With the rapid adoption of Geospatial Artificial Intelligence (Geo-AI), especially Geo-LLMs, it is crucial to develop frameworks for moderating such domain-specific LLMs. Furthermore, the significance of location data and emergence of high-resolution remote sensing data makes it imperative to focus on such developments. We present GeoSAFE-Dataset, a novel dataset consisting of safe and unsafe prompts, along with corresponding responses. The dataset is based on a new Geo-AI safety taxonomy comprising of hazard categories aligning with either the geospatial datasets or their applications. Additionally, we present evaluations based on zero-shot Llama Guard 3 and a fine-tuned model, named as GeoSAFE, for the proposed dataset. We see an improvement of 1.22x on the F1-score (0.797 → 0.973) and 1.15x improvement in Recall (0.841 → 0.975). Most importantly, we see an improvement of 7.3x in the FSR. Although, the GeoSAFE taxonomy is not complete and comprehensive, we are making an attempt to provide dataset and fine-tuned model so that reliable and responsible Geo-AI systems can be developed.
